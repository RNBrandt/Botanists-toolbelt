B.S., 1963, Sam Houston State Teachers College; A.M., 1964, Washington University, St. Louis; Ph.D., 1967, University of Texas.

Dr. John L. Strother is in charge of Compositae (Asteraceae) in University Herbarium (and curates the comps in JEPS as well). He also participates in matters concerning curation of other seed-plant collections and in public-service plant identifications for campus, municipal, state, and federal agencies, as well as for private and public institutions and universities.

In recent years, Dr. Strother's main research efforts have been dedicated to two large-scale undertakings:

1.   Production of a treatment of Compositae for a flora of Chiapas, Mexico. Chiapas is a floristically rich region that is poorly known and clearly in need of botanical expertise. Dr. Strother is treating all of the Compositae (ca. 200 genera, ca. 600 species) for the project. He has published a major part of the project (82 genera, 226 species, as: 1999. Compositae - Heliantheae s.l. Fl. Chiapas. Part 5. California Academy of Sciences).

2.   Production of treatments (descriptions, keys, discussions) of genera and species of Compositae for the Flora of North America North of Mexico (FNANM). In addition to his general coördination of treatments as one of three taxon-editors for Compositae, as a contributor to the flora, Dr. Strother wrote treatments for 119 genera (401 species) and co-wrote treatments of 23 genera (118 species). Most of the work was specimen-based and was done with collections housed here in JEPS and UC. In connection with preparation of treatments for FNANM, Dr. Strother visited herbaria housed at the Botanical Research Institute of Texas (BRIT, SMU, and VDB), at the California Academy of Sciences (CAS and DS), and at the University of Texas (LL and TEX). Dr. Strother is also on the Editorial Committee for the whole of FNANM and contributes to the editing of all volumes of the flora.


Dr. Strother sometimes leads seminars on botanical nomenclature in the Department of Integrative Biology at UC and, with Dr. Bruce G. Baldwin, leads Jepson workshops on Compositae.
