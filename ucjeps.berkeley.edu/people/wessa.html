I received my bachelor of art's in bacteriology from the University of California at Davis in 1984 and began a career in research which has included working with molecular biology, insect cell culture, protein chemistry and pathology for the biotechnology industry and for grant funded research positions. In 1993 I earned a professional clear secondary level teaching credential in Life Science and Chemistry from Sonoma State University.

I have been a staff research associate for the Jepson Curator, Dr. Bruce Baldwin, since 1995. I am responsible for all stages of providing data for phylogenetic analysis: DNA extraction, PCR amplification, sequence and data organization. Besides generating data for Dr. Baldwin's research I manage two molecular labs and train undergraduates, graduate students and postdoctoral researchers in our molecular techniques and other protocols pertinent to phylogenetics.

Recreational interests: When I'm not in the labs, I enjoy the lovely SF Bay Area, spending time with friends, gardening, birding, photography and hanging out with my sweet old cat.
