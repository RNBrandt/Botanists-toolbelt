This is a draft of a page meant to introduce beginners to the mosses and other nonvascular plants of California. If you already know what you're doing, go to the Home page of the California Moss eFlora. Please send suggested revisions to paul.wilson@csun.edu

We have great things planned for novices, some are well established, others are in the gray zone

▸ Introduction to bryophytes, Jepson Workshop: most years a weekend class is offered at Berkeley to get people started

▸ Foray: beginners are welcome to joins us for the spring foray, and we always have breakaway groups for beginners

▸ Videos

  • Jim Shevock on how Cal Academy is studying mosses etc.
  • Lena Coleman on mosses of Sequoia National Park: part 1, part 2, part 3

▸ Start reading:

  • Fremontia special issue: Bryophytes 31(3)
  • Gathering Moss, by Robin Wall Kimmerer
  • Introduction to Bryophytes, by Alain Vanderpoorten and Bernard Goffinet

▸ Handouts (work in progress): we are planning a series of full-color guides

  • Mosses Etc. of Sequoia National Park
